What This Solves

Your setup is
costing you before
you say a word.

Bad audio and a cluttered, confusing workflow are not minor issues. They affect how seriously people take what you're putting out — and how seriously you take yourself when you're making it.

Problem 01

The Wrong Setup for What You're Making

Muffled audio, blown-out lighting, a room that picks up every sound — these things don't just hurt quality, they make people stop watching or listening before you've had a chance to say anything. You don't need expensive equipment. You need the right equipment for what you're specifically making, set up correctly. That's what this session figures out.

Problem 02

No Clear Picture of What to Fix

You could spend hundreds on a new mic and still sound bad because the problem was the room, not the mic. You could buy a camera and never use it because your lighting makes everything look flat. This session gives you a clear picture of what's actually causing the issue and what to do about it — before you spend another dollar guessing.
